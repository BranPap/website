About Me

Originally from San Diego, California, I am a second-year PhD student of Linguistics at Stanford University. My research so far has primarily examined the intersections between music, identity, and language, though I have been known to stray somewhat into pragmatics, language acquisition, and morphology from time to time.

I graduated from the University of Edinburgh with a Masters of Arts with First Class Honours in Linguistics in 2020. During my time there, I served as the First Year Representative, Social Secretary, and President of the student Lingusitics Society. I also served as the National Chair of the Undergraduate Linguistics Association of Britain.

When I'm not doing linguistics, I can often be found reading (especially horror), playing video games (you can follow me on Twitch), or visting the theatre. I'm always down for a good coffee-and-books session!

My friends in California call me Bran or BranPap, and my friends in the UK call me BPap. I'm pretty much cool with whatever!
