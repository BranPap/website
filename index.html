I am a final-year undergraduate at the University of Edinburgh, where I have recently submitted my dissertation: Reconceptualising the Heritage Speaker: Toward a Dynamic, Multi-Dimensional, and Emic Profile of Heritage Speakers. A case study of Spanish in San Diego, California. I am currently serving as the National Chair of the Undergraduate Linguistics Association of Britain

In the autumn, I will begin studying towards a PhD student at Stanford University. I hope to focus my research on sociolinguistics and the linguistic constructions of personae in contemporary popular music. I also hope to expand into the study of linguistic perception of singing, focusing on misheard lyrics and how our social perceptions of musical artsists condition what we mishear.
